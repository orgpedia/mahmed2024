The proposed purchase rates are approximate when given administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. The Commissioner (Medical Education) as well as the Administrator concerned shall ensure that the rate is below the market price and does not exceed the rate at which the supplier concerned has supplied these items elsewhere.
The specifications of the equipment (1801109 specification) BMH, are comprehensive and accordingly the equipment supplier AHIR should be of such a type that three bids can be received from at least three manufacturers / suppliers. Also, the procurement process should be done only after verifying that the technical specification is not designed to benefit any particular company.
The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Dean concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any other proposal / purchase and that the equipment is required by the institution concerned.
While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounts provided for administrative approval.
It will be necessary to show the prices of the main equipment and their accessories as different.
The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be followed strictly while executing the entire procurement process.
