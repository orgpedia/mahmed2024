1) This administrative recognition is being provided to the Government Medical Colleges and Hospitals from the grants sanctioned and available under the State Schemes during the financial year 2026-27.
2) In this regard, Government Decision No.Bhankhas-2014 / No.82 / Part-I issued by the Department of Industries, Power and Labour regarding "Manual of Procedure for Office Procurement to be carried out by the Government Department." \\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\\ / Industry-4, dated 01.12.2016 and the prescribed procedure mentioned in the subsidiary government decisions should be complied with.
3) The proposed purchase rates are approximate when given administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. The Commissioner (Medical Education) as well as the Principal concerned shall ensure that the rate is less than the market price and not more than the rate at which the concerned supplier has supplied these items elsewhere.
4) The specifications of the equipment (801108 specification) BMH, are comprehensive and the equipment supply WHR should be of such a type that three tenders can be received from at least three manufacturers / suppliers. Also, the procurement process should be done only after verifying that the technical specification does not have a ready-made HSS that will benefit any particular company.
5) The Dean will be responsible for ensuring that the turnkey project is set up as specified.
6) The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Dean concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any proposal elsewhere / is not allowed to be purchased and that the equipment is of interest to the institution concerned.
7) While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it should be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounting provided for administrative approval.
8) It will be necessary to show the prices of the main equipment and their accessories as different.
9) Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) guidelines should be strictly followed while executing the entire procurement process.
