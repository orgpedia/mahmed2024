Introduction: Nursing is considered an essential and indispensable part of the global health system.
While the population is growing rapidly in a developing country like India, the needs for healthcare are growing exponentially. In such a situation, the demand for trained manpower in the nursing field is constantly increasing and the number of graduates currently available is becoming insufficient in comparison to this demand. There are currently 35 medical colleges and affiliated hospitals in the state. Hospitals affiliated to these colleges as well as private hospitals in the state also require trained nurses.
The state currently (as of 2025) has 2,17,640 registered nurses and 5 nursing degrees (8.Sc.) of student capacity affiliated to government medical colleges, keeping in view the requirement of health care nurses. The government was considering to goo the student intake capacity of the nursing colleges, upgrade 8 GNM diploma colleges to start nursing degree colleges with an intake capacity of 100 students, and set up new government nursing degree colleges with an intake capacity of 100 students affiliated to government medical colleges. The proposal was mooted on 21.6.2026. The approval of the Cabinet has been received and the Government has accordingly taken the following decision.
