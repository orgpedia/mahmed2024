3. The proposed purchase rates are approximate when given administrative approval. Hence, the prices should be fixed at the end of the procurement process. The Commissioner (Medical Education) as well as the Administrator concerned shall ensure that the rate is below the market price and does not exceed the rate at which the supplier concerned has supplied these items elsewhere.
4. The fattest (technical specification) of the equipment should be broad, comprehensive and accordingly three bids should be received from at least three manufacturers / suppliers who can supply the equipment. Also, these items should be processed only after verifying that the technical specification is not a ready-made HSS that will benefit any particular company.
5. The Commissioner (Medical Education) and the Dean concerned shall ensure that the proposed equipment is not included in any proposal elsewhere / is not allowed to be purchased and that the equipment is required by the institution concerned.
6. It will be necessary to show the prices of the main equipment and their accessories as different.
7. It should be ensured that no new designations are required for the equipment.
8. While deciding on the purchase of machinery in this regard, it shall be ensured that the expenditure does not exceed the provision of grant-in-aid sanctioned under the head of accounts provided for administrative approval.
